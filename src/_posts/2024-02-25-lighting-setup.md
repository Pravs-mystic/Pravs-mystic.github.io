It's called Lamy Safari. I never even heard of that brand before.

I am currently holding a Sigma Micron fine line pen. Pravalli, my dear wife got me this pen for me when she was coming from India to the USA for the first time. I cherish these pens. She brought me a set of pens. Each pen has a different color. I don't like losing these pens, so I don't let Pravalli borrow them.

After writing the above paragraph, I cleaned my desk. I tried to make my desk optimized for a good reading and writing experience. It's clean now and less cluttered. I disconnected my keyboard to get more space. I am currently using the Moonlander Mark 1 keyboard. It's big! Occupies a lot of space on my desk. It's nice to see all the space freed up.

The lighting situation on my desk is not good. I have two lights in the bedroom. "Skylight" and "bedlight", Those are the names assigned to the lights. They work well as ambient lights. They light up the room but my desk surface is not properly lit for reading a book. I rarely read a hard copy book, but when I do, I read it in the living room where the lighting is good. I recently started reading the hard copy version of the book "The Psychology of Money". I enjoy reading the book and I would prefer reading the book at my desk. So I want to fix the lighting situation at my desk.

To fix the lighting, I am using a combination of four items.
1. skylight's pole
1. bike headlight
1. old phone mount for my car
1. velcro strap

![](/img/three-items.jpeg)

Skylight is taller than me. Its pole is made of a ferromagnetic substance. The light is situated towards the left of my desk and it always points to the top. I can make it direct to the table surface.

The bike headlight is nice and bright. I rarely use it on the bike. So if I'm going to use it on my desk, I need to have a way to attach and detach it both on my bike and near my desk frequently.

The phone mount is a very odd looking product. When I showed this item alone, nobody was able to figure out the purpose of the product. It has a strong flat magnet that attaches to a phone and a flat tongue like protrusion that attaches or hooks into the AC vent of the car.

![](/img/phone-mount.jpg)

The lighting setup solution was to put the headlight flat on the vent hook/tongue of the phone mount and secure it with the velcro. and use the magnet to attach the phone mount to the pole of the skylight. 

![](/img/combine.jpeg)

I can use the ball and socket mechanism on the phone mount to target the surface on my desk. What a bonus! I can also freely adjust the height of the light because the magnet just slides along the height of the pole! Isn't that amazing!!!
